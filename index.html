Is Artifical Intelligence Becoming More Human?

(A Sentiment Analysis of Film Characters)

The ability to convey and comprehend emotions through language is a profound reflection of human intelligence. After Alan Turing proposed in 1950 that computers could exhibit intelligence, a series of intriguing questions emerged in the decades that followed: Does this include 'emotional intelligence'? Can computers genuinely express and experience emotions? Under what circumstances might computers use their emotional intelligence? How can we assess emotional intelligence in machines?

The introduction of Chat GPT in 2022 has drawn considerable attention to large language models and their usage and simplification of the human language. While these models excel in their ability to generate text, they do so without the emotional nuances that are inherent in human communication. This raises a new series of questions regarding the future of human-computer relationships and the future of the human language.

Humans often turn to science fiction films to explore the potential of artificial intelligence (AI). The idea of “robots taking over the world” has long been a recurring theme. However, as capabilities of AI expanded in the real world, so did their roles in movies. Initially portrayed as villains, as exemplified in 2001: A Space Odyssey (1968), AI characters later transitioned into romantic interests, as seen in the films Her (2013) and Ex Machina (2014).

Both Her and Ex Machina touch upon the human tendency to anthropomorphize machines and the ethical implications of granting moral consideration to AI. These films both involve humans using technology for their emotional needs, which is a comment and a prediction of how technology will use our own language to emotionally connect with us, whether that be for romantic or social reasons. However, current large language models are devoid of emotion, so is it fair to call this technology a ‘language’ model?

To examine if emotions are present in the language of machines, I used a sentiment analysis library in Python to measure the emotional spectrum of the human male character (Caleb) and the female AI character (Ava) in Ex Machina. Sentiment analysis is a natural language processing technique used to determine the sentiment or emotional tone expressed in a piece of text, such as a review, social media post, or comment. It is a limited field as of now, but this particular library is able to detect five emotions from text: happiness, sadness, anger, surprise, and fear.

While it’s important to note that Ex Machina is a work of fiction, and the analysis tool itself is AI-driven, this examination serves as a lens through which we can contemplate potential implications.

While the chart shows that Ava experienced the emotions, it’s more important to note the reciprocity of emotions between the characters. Caleb experienced heightened emotions in sync with Ava. During their moments together, the human found himself not only attracted to the AI character, but he was also able to form an emotional bond with her, as they found common ground in their experiences of loneliness. In this intriguing dynamic, we witness how technology is evolving to comprehend human language, interpret human emotions, and communicate with us.

Caleb’s empathy and vulnerability with Ava also reflects a broader trend in our relationship with technology. Our digital language and behavior, including both human-to-human communication online and our interactions with technology, constitutes a unique form of communication In which we convey our emotional needs and vulnerabilities. For example, social media posts may reflect desires for belonging, while scrolling through feeds can serve as a means of addressing loneliness or boredom. Technology responds to our needs, such as through app recommendations and personalized shopping suggestions. Online games communicate with players through audio, visuals, and feedback, creating an interactive and immersive experience.

As technology advances its ways to communicate with us and utilize the human language, our relationship with the machines evolves, igniting a reciprocal relationship. This shift is particularly pronounced in a post-Covid society where remote work has normalized solitude and loneliness levels have peaked. Loneliness and our emotions is a vulnerability that corporations can tap into it, such as through dating apps. We are increasingly allowing AI to passively infiltrate our lives and exploit our emotions. The progression of science and technology raises the question: who bears the responsibility for this evolving dynamic? Well the end of Ex Machina (Ava was able to manipulate Caleb’s empathy) proves that the AI itself has no moral responsibility to fulfill human’s emotional needs, as Ava was able to manipulate it, breaking our trust with technology.

AI's ability to decipher the full spectrum of human emotions remains a work in progress, challenging researchers and developers to refine their technology continually. It is hard to achieve considering the psychological and behavioral differences between people, as well as the evolution of our language. AI has to keep up with new phrases and unique emotional connotations that may emerge. The problem with researchers also lies in their own ability to figure out the human brain, which is continually a work in progress and is why there is even progress with AI itself - to figure out ourselves. Creating the algorithm containing all patterns of being a human seems impossible now, but as the machine learning continues, in the future they might be able to translate that complexity to codes and figures.

This seemed like something of science fiction movies, but the future becomes more uncertain the more comfortable we become with technology (the possibilities widen). The more we express our emotions with technology, the more we are extricably tied to technology. This may have an effect on the human language, the way we perceive our emotions, and our communication systems offline. It also will effect our needs, and the way that technology can fulfill those emotional needs or take on new roles connected to our emotions (fix). In this evolving landscape, the interplay between AI and human emotions becomes a topic of both promise and uncertainty, ushering us into a future where technology seeks to comprehend the deepest layers of human expression while acknowledging the vast uncharted territories that remain.
